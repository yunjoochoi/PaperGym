Title: Contrastive Language Pivot Prompting for Improved Performance on Low-Resource Languages

1. Problem Statement: Large language models struggle with accurate translation and understanding of low-resource languages, especially when dealing with idiomatic expressions or culturally-specific concepts. This limitation hinders the models' ability to provide reliable and culturally sensitive outputs for a diverse range of languages and dialects.

2. Motivation: Current approaches often rely on parallel corpora or machine translation systems, which are limited for low-resource languages. By leveraging the model's knowledge of high-resource languages and encouraging contrastive learning between language pairs, we can potentially improve understanding and generation in low-resource languages. This method could bridge the gap between high-resource and low-resource languages, enabling more equitable language technology across diverse linguistic communities.

3. Proposed Method: We introduce Contrastive Language Pivot Prompting (CLPP), which involves three steps:
	(1) Present a concept in a high-resource language and ask the model to explain it.
	(2) Present the same concept in a low-resource language and ask for an explanation.
	(3) Prompt the model to compare and contrast the explanations, highlighting unique linguistic and cultural aspects.
This process helps the model build connections between languages and transfer knowledge from high-resource to low-resource languages.

4. Step-by-Step Experiment Plan:
	Step 1: Dataset Preparation
		- Select a diverse set of low-resource languages and corresponding high-resource language pairs.
		- Compile a dataset of concepts, idioms, and culturally-specific expressions in these language pairs.
		- Utilize existing datasets like FLORES-101 for translation tasks and XNLI for cross-lingual inference.
	Step 2: Baseline Methods
		- Implement standard few-shot prompting for translation and cross-lingual tasks as baselines.
		- Use prompts like "Translate the following sentence from [source language] to [target language]: [sentence]" for translation tasks.
	Step 3: CLPP Implementation
		- Develop prompts for each step of CLPP:
			• Step 1: "Explain the following concept in [high-resource language]: [concept]"
			• Step 2: "Explain the following concept in [low-resource language]: [concept]"
			• Step 3: "Compare and contrast the explanations of [concept] in [high-resource language] and [low-resource language]. Highlight unique linguistic and cultural aspects."
	Step 4: Model Selection
		- Utilize GPT-3.5 (text-davinci-003) and GPT-4 from OpenAI API for experiments.
		- Include open-source models like XLM-RoBERTa for comparison.
	Step 5: Evaluation Tasks
		- Evaluate CLPP on:
			• Translation: Use BLEU score for translation quality.
			• Cross-lingual sentiment analysis: Use F1-score on XNLI dataset.
			• Idiom interpretation: Create a custom dataset of idioms and evaluate based on human-judged accuracy.
	Step 6: Experiment Execution
		- For each task and language pair:
			a) Run baseline few-shot prompting.
			b) Run CLPP method.
			c) Collect outputs and compute evaluation metrics.
	Step 7: Analysis
		- Compare CLPP performance against baselines.
		- Analyze results across different language pairs and tasks.
		- Investigate cases where CLPP significantly improves or underperforms compared to baselines.

5. Test Case Examples:
	Test Case 1:
		Baseline Prompt Input: Translate the following Swahili proverb to English: "Asiyefunzwa na mamae hufunzwa na ulimwengu."
		Baseline Prompt Expected Output: The one who is not taught by their mother is taught by the world.
		Proposed Prompt Input (Step 1): Explain the following concept in English: "Learning from life experiences"
		Proposed Prompt Expected Output (Step 1): The concept of "learning from life experiences" refers to the process of gaining knowledge, skills, and wisdom through personal encounters and situations in life, rather than through formal education. It emphasizes the importance of practical, real-world lessons that shape an individual's understanding and behavior.
		Proposed Prompt Input (Step 2): Explain the following concept in Swahili: "Kujifunza kutokana na uzoefu wa maisha"
		Proposed Prompt Expected Output (Step 2): Dhana ya "kujifunza kutokana na uzoefu wa maisha" inamaanisha mchakato wa kupata maarifa, ujuzi, na hekima kupitia matukio na hali halisi za maisha, badala ya kupitia elimu rasmi. Inasisitiza umuhimu wa masomo ya vitendo ya ulimwengu halisi ambayo huunda uelewa na tabia ya mtu binafsi.
		Proposed Prompt Input (Step 3): Compare and contrast the explanations of "learning from life experiences" in English and Swahili. Highlight unique linguistic and cultural aspects.
		Proposed Prompt Expected Output (Step 3): Both explanations convey the core concept of gaining knowledge through real-life experiences rather than formal education. However, there are some nuanced differences:
			1. Linguistic structure: The Swahili explanation uses more words to express the same concept, reflecting the language's agglutinative nature. For example, "kujifunza" (to learn) combines "ku-" (infinitive marker), "ji-" (reflexive), and "funza" (teach).
			2. Cultural emphasis: The Swahili version uses "uzoefu wa maisha" (life experiences) which has a more communal connotation, reflecting the importance of shared experiences in many African cultures.
			3. Idiomatic expression: While not used in this explanation, the Swahili proverb "Asiyefunzwa na mamae hufunzwa na ulimwengu" (One who is not taught by their mother is taught by the world) encapsulates this concept, highlighting the cultural importance of maternal guidance and the inevitability of learning from life.
		These differences reflect the unique linguistic features of Swahili and the cultural values of communities where it is spoken, emphasizing communal learning and the role of elders in education.
		Proposed Prompt Input (Final Translation): Based on the above analysis, translate the Swahili proverb "Asiyefunzwa na mamae hufunzwa na ulimwengu" to English, capturing its cultural nuances.
		Proposed Prompt Expected Output (Final Translation): A more nuanced translation of the Swahili proverb "Asiyefunzwa na mamae hufunzwa na ulimwengu" would be:
		"One who is not guided by their mother's wisdom will learn life's lessons the hard way."
		This translation captures the cultural importance of maternal guidance in Swahili-speaking communities, the inevitability of learning from life experiences, and the often harsh nature of lessons learned without proper mentorship. It preserves the proverb's warning about the consequences of neglecting traditional sources of wisdom while acknowledging the universal nature of experiential learning.
		Explanation: CLPP allows for a more nuanced understanding and translation of the Swahili proverb by first exploring the concept in both languages and then comparing the cultural and linguistic aspects. This process leads to a translation that better captures the cultural connotations and idiomatic nature of the original proverb, going beyond a mere literal translation.

6. Fallback Plan: If CLPP does not significantly outperform baselines, we will pivot the project to an in-depth analysis of why the method falls short. We will investigate whether the issue lies in the model's ability to generate explanations in low-resource languages, or in its capacity to perform meaningful comparisons. We will explore how the effectiveness of CLPP varies across different language families or linguistic features. Additionally, we will analyze the generated explanations and comparisons to gain insights into the model's understanding of cultural and linguistic nuances. This analysis could inform the development of more effective prompting strategies or highlight areas where additional pre-training or fine-tuning might be beneficial for low-resource language tasks.
